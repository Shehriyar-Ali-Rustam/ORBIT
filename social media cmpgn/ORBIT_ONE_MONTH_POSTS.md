DAY 10 · WEDNESDAY · TEACH SOMETHING
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Your website is slow. And it is costing you customers.

Here is the part nobody tells you:

A 1 second delay in page load time causes a 7 percent drop in conversions.
If your website makes $10,000 per month, you are losing $700 for every second of delay.

The main reasons websites are slow:

Images are not optimised.
Most websites serve images at 3x the size they need to be.
Fix: compress every image before uploading. Use WebP format.

No caching.
Every visitor re-downloads the same files from scratch.
Fix: use a CDN so files are served from a server near the user.

Too many plugins.
Every WordPress plugin adds code that runs on every page load.
Fix: remove plugins you do not actively use. Consider moving to a modern framework.

Render blocking scripts.
JavaScript files that load before the page is visible.
Fix: move scripts to the bottom of the page or load them asynchronously.

No performance monitoring.
You cannot fix what you cannot measure.
Fix: run your website through PageSpeed Insights right now. It is free.

A fast website is not a luxury. It is a business decision.

What score does your website get on PageSpeed Insights? 👇

DAY 24 · WEDNESDAY · TEACH SOMETHING
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Most people use AI wrong.

They open ChatGPT, type a vague question, get a vague answer, and decide AI is not that useful.

Here is how to actually get remarkable results from any AI tool:

Give it a role first.
Instead of "write me an email" say "You are a senior sales executive with 15 years of experience. Write a follow up email to a client who has not responded in one week."
The output is completely different.

Give it context.
The more specific information you provide, the better the answer.
Tell it who you are, what you are trying to achieve, and who your audience is.

Tell it what format you want.
"Give me 5 bullet points" gets a different response than "write this as a short paragraph."
Always specify the format before you ask the question.

Tell it what to avoid.
"Do not use corporate jargon. Do not use bullet points. Do not be longer than 100 words."
Constraints produce better results than open ended requests.

Ask it to improve its own answer.
After it responds, say "now make this 30 percent shorter and more direct."
The second version is almost always better than the first.

AI is not a magic button. It is a tool that rewards clear thinking.

The better you are at asking questions, the better AI performs for you.

DAY 29 · TEACH SOMETHING EXTRA
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Every developer should understand one business concept above all others.

Customer acquisition cost.

It sounds complicated. It is not.

It simply means: how much does it cost you to get one paying customer?

If you spend $100 on ads and get 10 customers, your acquisition cost is $10 per customer.
If each customer pays you $50, you made $40 profit per customer.
If each customer pays you $8, you are losing money with every single sale.

This one number changes every business decision:

It tells you whether your marketing is working.
It tells you what you can afford to spend on ads.
It tells you whether your pricing makes sense.
It tells you which channels are worth your time.

Most businesses that fail do not fail because of a bad product.
They fail because the cost of getting customers is higher than what customers pay them.

Understanding this one concept will make you a better developer, a better founder,
and a better advisor to any client you work with.
